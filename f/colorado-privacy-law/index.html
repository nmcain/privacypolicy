Overview of Policy Proposals and Solutions

What policy proposals and solutions are currently seen in the United States?

Executive order on safe, secure, and trustworthy AI

  • Expresses the potential for "promise and peril" surrounding AI technology
  • Explains eight governing principles that the United States will use to be leading the growing technology, in a responsible way
  • Specifically wanted to protect workers and civil rights, as well as the protection of American's privacy

American Data Privacy and Protection Act (ADPPA)

  • Potential to be the country's first bipartisan bill to create a comprehensive law surrounding American citizens' privacy

Cloud Act 

  • Requires law enforcement to have a warrant when asking data companies for access to a customer's data, specifically in other countries 
  • The act has raised concerns about safety of data stored in the cloud, and has conflicts with regulations in the GDPR 

Colorado Privacy Law

  • Allows Colorado Consumers with the explicit rights to access, delete and correct their personal data
  • Extra protections regarding the collection of data were also instituted, including running protection assessments and needing consent before processing sensitive data

Illinois Data Privacy and Protection Act

  • Introduced to the Illionois House in 2023, would limit the amount of data that companies could collect to  perform the task the consumer is expecting 
  • Focused on data minimization, while mapped of the ADPPA, would create some of the strongest legislation currently introduced in the US

California Consumer Privacy Act

  • Implemented in 2018 and updated in 2023
  • Established six core rights for Californians 
  • One of the first state policies to go into effect

EU General Data Protection Regulation

  • Inspired US privacy legislation
  • European Union law for how organizations and companies can use data they collect
  • Wanted to ensure integrity among the companies and data minimization 

Big Policies

Where the U.S. may take Privacy Policy

What We Think

We believe that the United States is moving in the right direction as far as policy and legislation to regulate the collection of consumers data. Due to the history surrounding the protection of privacy, stemming from the Fourth Amendment, we feel that this extension of privacy is extremely important to ensure that it is explicitly protected, especially as we move into a time of great reliance and usage of all kinds of technology. The ADPPA seems to be a cohesive bill proposal and we hope to see legislation like this passed in the near future. 

Why We Think This

From looking at the benefits and protections provided by the European GDPR, we can hope that a policy like this would ensure that American citizens are guaranteed a right to their online data and privacy. Since the ADPPA was also able to gain bipartisan support in the house, this leads us to believe that there is support from both parties, meaning that this is an issue that is being acknowledged on a broad scale. 

What is Still Needed?

If the ADPPA has a chance of passing both the House and Senate, there will surely be countless amendments and changes to it before it passes and seeks presidential approval, as there are with most bills. We feel that in this time between approval of either the ADPPA or a bill similar to it, there needs to be more awareness provided to American citizens about their privacy risks, shifting public opinion to meet the policy window. The issue of online and data privacy is one that will continue to grow across generations so ensuring that Americans are educated on their risks and rights will help to ensure we are progressing in a positive manner as a society.
